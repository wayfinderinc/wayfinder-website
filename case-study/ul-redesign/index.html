UL.com Enterprise Redesign

Collaboration and flexibility are key to success

Case Study—Information Architecture, Content Strategy, UX/UI Design

Scope

Wayfinder participated in the redesign of UL.com from 2017 through 2018. The redesign restructured how UL presents itself to its customers, allowing easier view of their myriad of services, while allowing both their current and potential customers an easier avenue of engaging with UL.

The redesign was fueled by a concurrent brand refresh, restructure of UL's service catalog, thought leadership platform definition, and a unification of UL's content strategy across multiple markets and domains.

Who is UL?

UL LLC is a global safety certification company headquartered in Northbrook, Illinois. Established in 1894 UL has expanded into an organization with 64 laboratories, testing and certification facilities serving customers in 104 countries.

UL works with businesses, industries, governments, regulatory authorities to keep professionals and the public safe.

Digital Transformation

UL's success is also one of its main challenges. Since its transformation into a for-profit company in 2012, UL has worked to diversify its services to meet an ever-changing marketplace.

The company has evolved from its roots in electrical and fire safety to address broader safety issues such as hazardous substances, water quality, food safety, performance testing, safety and compliance education and environmental sustainability.

As an already broad service catalog continues to grow UL's existing content strategy and digital architecture struggled to communicate with UL's customers effectively.

Wayfinder joins internal and multi agency partners as part of planning sessions at UL's Global Headquarters in Northbrook, IL.

Audiences

Understanding our audiences is critical to our ability to define, design, and develop solutions that are aligned with their needs and expectations. UL has a broad set of audiences with diverse needs and expectations, challenging our ability to use what we know about them to define navigation and content.

The risk in pursuing a strategy of being everything to everyone results in a website that is difficult to navigate and lacks clarity or focus. This is why we have reframed how UL thinks about users, transitioning away from personas to user dimensions.

Internal Audiences

Our internal audiences consist of +10,000 employees and include anyone who has a role in directly or indirectly providing our clients with services or operating our business. The needs and expectations of our internal audiences were grouped by specific job functions.

External Audiences

Our external audiences come to UL.com to find the information to help them accomplish a goal but the information they seek varies greatly based on their industry, organization, business needs, role, job function, familiarity with UL, and their mode, intent, or their phase in the development cycle (if engaged in product, service, or location development and management).

Offerings Strategy

Offerings serve as the core building blocks of service offerings, bundles, collections, etc. Before the redesign, Services missed opportunities with existing and potential clients by not communicating from a customer-centric point-of-view.

By packaging services from the customer's viewpoint UL enables it's customers to find relavant Offerings more easily. The Offerings have become a sourced data point accessible by anywhere on UL.com, supporting related topics based on content mappings corss referenced by categorization and tagging.

Headless CMS

With the implementation of the platform and CAPE model the paradigm for creating content changed. Content moved to structured content freely available by any resource, enabling more content curation and less traditional walled gardens owned by specific business units.

  • Move from unstructured to structured content (BLOB HTML** vs data objects)
  • Move from page ownership to content authoring, curation & sharing (headless content)
  • Introduce enterprise-wide standardized rules for all of our public content domains
  • Create "Content Experience Centers", centralized virtual hubs to aggregate all content from a given content domain at UL and allow it to be shared across multiple digital properties
  • Move from 'One Size Fits All' to 'Connected Journeys' (Enterprise + Targeted Views*)

Information Architecture

The larger experience and design teams held multiple working session in order to nail down the restructured architecture. The architecture sought to reduce the number of clicks to find a piece of content, elimnate low performing pages and consoldate content where applicable.

Design

UL's enterprise brand refresh sought a cleaner, modern look that was humanized through photography and core messaging. Knowing the broad audience the site balanced enabling audiences to quickly find relevant topics and Highlighting lesser known but strategic information throughout the site so customers gain awareness of UL’s broad capabilities.

Supporting the UL Digital Platform’s ability to leverage atomized content (the practice of breaking down one broader theme or a larger piece of content into smaller, focused and more strategic content) required a shift in the ways that UL authors its content. This strategy enables the enterprise team to:

  • Create reusable content
  • Elevate content based on situation and topic
  • Reduce the noise
  • Centralize customer content by bringing like content together easily
  • Partner with complimentary programs

Navigation

The navigation sought to simplify UL's complex structure while still enabling customers to dive into specific content. Using a series of mega menus each navigation items is easily browsable from anywhere on the site.

Precise Communication

Each navigation item was precisely and meticulously chosen based on user testing with a technical audience. The result is a simplified navigation structure that displays the least amount of copy necessary.

Responsive

Modern customers expect access to content everywhere and anywhere. UL.com provides the enterprise with a quick and easy to understand mobile navigation and powerful search tools - even while on-the-go.

Homepage

The homepage sets the context of the entire website for any user. All major content types are represented on the homepage, with example content to provide substance for the user.

From Left to Right, an iterative exploration of the homepage based on stakeholder feedback and user testing.

Offerings

Organizing our strategy based on Customer Needs allow UL to speak to its capabilities from the Enterprise or Master Brand level. All other organizing principles (Industry, Specialization, Packaged Collection, core Offering, etc) are horizontally integrated underneath this need.

Broad to Narrow Focus

Each template in Offerings clearly explains the page's main purpose, what actions it provides the customer, then providing supporting topical resources.

Filter and Sort

The control bar collects all refinement and pagination controls. These controls allow the customer to quickly refine the hub page’s contents by identifying topics, industry, markets, and type of content.

Impact

UL.com recently launched, updated on its new content platform, modernized content strategy, supported by a refreshed brand. The team has received postive feedback from customers and internal audeiences alike.

This is the first phase of a multiyear rollout that will refresh all of UL's many touchpoints. Internal teams continue to gather feedback from internal teams and their customers, working to optimize content, offerings, and thought leadership.
